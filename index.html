Chicken Satay

This week's recipe is Satay Ayam, a special dish from Indonesian

Origins and history

The dish first appreared around 1870 in Java, Indonesia from photographs of satay sellers.
In 1917, was called "satai" in English when referring to "satai" seller in Singapore.
In Denpasar, Bali around 1937 it was known as "saté", then "satay" around 1955 while being associated with Malays cooking.

Although there are many claims and variations of satay, the dish is believed to have existed since the 15th century in East Java, Indonesia. Due to immigration and trading, by the late 19th century, satay spread to Indonesia neighbours, Malaysia, Singapore and Thailand.

Sate Ayam

Grilling Sate

Ingredients

Sate marinade
1 lb. pork shoulder or chicken meat (thigh and breast meat)
3 dashes garlic salt
paprika
1 dashes ground white pepper
2 teaspoons ground coriander
ground nutmeg
1 teaspoon ground cumin
olive oil
soy sauce
2 cloves shallot, thinly sliced
1/2 cup Kecap Manis or Indonesian sweet soy sauce
Sauce for grilling
1/4 cup Kecap Manis or Indonesian sweet soy sauce
1 teaspoon fish sauce
1 clove shallot, thinly sliced
2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
Peanut sauce
1 cup peanuts or bottled crunchy peanut butter
1 tablespoon olive oil
1 clove garlic
2 cloves shallot
1 teaspoon ground cumin
2 teaspoons ground ground coriander
3 dahses ground white pepper
2-3 tablespoons palm suger or brown suger

Instructions

Sate
1 Cut your meat into bite sized cubes, then marinate with all the Sate Marinade ingredients.
2 Marinate for at least 2 hours, but best overnight in the fridge.
Skewering sate
1 Pre-soak the bamboo skewers with water overnight.
2 Assemble 4 - 5 pieces of the meat on each bamboo skewer. Mix fatty and lean meat cubes in each skewer. Repeat until you use up all the meat.
Grilling and basting sate
1 Grill the sate over charcoal fire. While grilling, brush the Basting Mixture on the both sides of the sate. The sate are done when you see burnt marks on both sides of the sate.
2 Grill the sate in a preheated oven at 200°C (392°F) by placing the sate skewers on a baking sheet for about 15 minutes, or until you see burnt marks on the sate. Brush the Basting Mixture on the sate before grilling in the oven. Turn the sate over and baste the mixture on the other side of the sate and continue grilling.
Peanut sauce
1 Process all the ingredients of the Peanut Sauce in a food processor until well combined.
Serving
1 Place the Peanut Sauce on a plate, add a dash of fish sauce, drizzle generously with kecap manis, and squeeze juice of a lime. Mix well. Add one of two freshly chopped red chilies in the Peanut Sauce, if you like.
